Art Deco Turing: Matt Ko

Design

The overarching theme of this webpage derives from the Art Deco era of design, historically around the 1920's. Even though this style is quite diverse, and you can find it in many places across the United States and in media. Think of the Great Gatsby film or Miami Beach architecture for example. An Art Deco theme features many simple, clean and streamlined shapes along with repeating geometric patterns. The style is associated with luxury and colors reflect that, in terms of metallic golds or silvers that accent neutral, natural colors that came with the use of various stone or concrete for construction.

This website reflects many of the features of an Art Deco design. The colors, a neutral Jade green backdrop with gold and light silver accents give off a pleasing but not too flashy design. The background has a repeating leaf pattern present in many Art Deco designs as well. The text boxes, tables, input boxes, submit buttons, etc. are arranged in a geometric and repeating style. Other aspects such as the font and borders unify the design as well.

Features

Beginning with the Home Page, the set up is horizontal, which differs from the default vertical scroll. The material for each date is separated into equal sized square blocks. The blocks themselves can be scrolled on if the information takes up more space than the block is given. The repeating geometric shape of the blocks coincides with many art deco designs. The text boxes are similarly placed in the prep form, grade form, and grade view. Overall, the design features a touchpad friendly design for laptops and a set up where most information is easily viewable. Swiping through the home page and watching the blocks pass by is almost addicting. On the homepage, for example, there is no need to scroll to see what needs to be done for the current or prep day, and using the scrollbar with a mouse does not take a significant amount of time longer to use. The size of the blocks and font sizes allow the user to easily type and read as well. The simple structure of this design provides the information with a body that is easy to interpret and manipulate, while also being luxurious and tasteful, a key aspect of Art Deco design.

  • Touchpad Friendly
  • Information front and center
  • Cohesive Art Deco Design
  • Simple and Clean
